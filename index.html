FLN Research Implementation

Achieving FLN with ALfA takes just 45 days. We invite governments to begin their 45-day FLN research implementation, enabling children under 10 to achieve foundational literacy and numeracy skills—reading, writing, and arithmetic—at 10 times the usual speed.

  • Select the geography or number of schools where you want to start implementation
  • Conduct a Benchmark Assessment / Pre-Test.
  • Train Teachers and FLN Managers, both offline and online.
  • Print the ALfA materials.
  • Implement the program for 45 working days.
  • Conduct a Benchmark Assessment / Post-Test.
  • Scale-up based on the results.

HARVARD ON ALfA FLN

Harvard Graduate School of Education, USA

Policy Pathways for Improving Foundational Literacy and Numeracy in Uttar Pradesh, India

in “Rebuilding Resilient Education Systems After the COVID-19 Pandemic”, March 2023, Amazon

ALfA: Accelerating Learning for All breaks the shackles of the industrial education system, revolutionizing the school experience through peer learning and hands-on activities. The ALfA program enables children and adults alike to learn foundational literacy and numeracy in months rather than years. Beyond reading, writing and arithmetic, ALfA builds the crucial life skills of collaboration, creativity, citizenship and character.

Both a classroom guide for teachers and an implementation roadmap for policymakers, this book explores a transformative approach to teaching and learning. Sprinkled with anecdotes from teachers, principals & students, Accelerating Learning for All inspires and equips us to build a better education system.”

President & MD

City Montessori School, Lucknow World’s Largest School Recipient UNESCO Prize for Peace Education

As the largest school in the world, City Montessori School (CMS), has always upheld the banner of quality education and has promoted an education based on principles of equity and excellence.

A K12 school in the city Lucknow, India, CMS started in 1959 with 5 children and the equivalent of 10 dollars in borrowed capital by Drs. Jagdish and Bharti Gandhi. The school was entered in the Guinness Book of World Records in 1999 and has since maintained its position as the world's largest school with the current enrollment of 61,000 children. CMS has promoted an education for peace and world unity. It received the UNESCO Prize for Peace Education in 2002. Children and staff greet each other "Jai Jagat! ‘Let glory be for the entire world’!

CMS hosts the Conference of Chief Justices of the World.

FLN Landscape and Research Trends

Explore the current FLN research landscape, emphasizing the imperative of research for SDG4 and Foundational Literacy and Numeracy (FLN). Delve into the latest trends, challenges, and transformative opportunities shaping reading, writing, and arithmetic skills.

Discuss the seamless integration of research findings into impactful classroom practices. Explore demonstrations and discussions on leveraging technology to narrow the digital divide in FLN.

Strategize diversity, equity, and inclusion in educational settings, particularly focusing on the education of girls and women. Emphasize evidence-based FLN practices with a focus on scalability and sustainability

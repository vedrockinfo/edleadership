Join Us in Pioneering Change

The crisis in education is a crisis of pedagogy. With the clock ticking on Sustainable Development Goal 4 (SDG4), achieving quality education feels distant, especially with unresolved foundational literacy and numeracy (FLN) issues.

We urgently need a transformative, evidence-supported pedagogy that can address FLN quickly and broadly. This approach should be holistic, inclusive, and seamlessly integrate climate change education into the curriculum.

Such a pedagogy must fit easily into existing systems, ensuring no child is left behind. It's a bold vision, but essential to making quality education a reality for all.

President & MD

City Montessori School (CMS) in Lucknow, India, stands as the world's largest school, known for its commitment to quality education based on equity and excellence.

Founded in 1959 by Drs. Jagdish and Bharti Gandhi with just five children and ten dollars in borrowed capital, CMS has grown tremendously. Today, it serves 61,000 students, earning its place in the Guinness Book of World Records in 1999.

CMS is dedicated to promoting education that fosters peace and world unity. In recognition of these efforts, CMS was awarded the UNESCO Prize for Peace Education in 2002. The school's greeting, "Jai Jagat!"—which means "Let glory be for the entire world!"—embodies its global vision.

CMS also hosts the Conference of Chief Justices of the World, an event that gathers legal leaders to discuss and promote peace through law. This conference underscores CMS's commitment to fostering international dialogue and justice.

FLN Research Implementation

Achieving FLN with ALfA takes just 45 days. We invite governments to begin their 45-day FLN research implementation, enabling children under 10 to achieve foundational literacy and numeracy skills—reading, writing, and arithmetic—at 10 times the usual speed.

  • Select the geography or number of schools where you want to start implementation
  • Conduct a Benchmark Assessment / Pre-Test.
  • Train Teachers and FLN Managers, both offline and online.
  • Print the ALfA materials.
  • Implement the program for 45 working days.
  • Conduct a Benchmark Assessment / Post-Test.
  • Scale-up based on the results.

HARVARD ON ALfA FLN

Harvard Graduate School of Education, USA

Policy Pathways for Improving Foundational Literacy and Numeracy in Uttar Pradesh, India

in “Rebuilding Resilient Education Systems After the COVID-19 Pandemic”, March 2023, Amazon

Chapter 8 of the book by Harvard Graduate School of Education (HGSE), Cambridge, USA, published in March 2023 documents the ALfA program in Shamli, and recommends its widespread adoption in UP.

Chapters 8 also documents results from the ALfA program from a Randomized Control Trial (RCT) conducted in the Shamli district of Uttar Pradesh. The RCT demonstrates a significant effect size of 0.23 for Grade 3 and 0.89 for Grade 5.

Chapter 10 of the same book is about the introduction of ALfA program at the nationwide level in the Republic of Maldives.

Publications & Media

A Groundbreaking Pedagogy

ACCELERATING LEARNING FOR ALL

“A much-needed manifesto for achieving large scale transformation of the global literacy crisis.”

ALfA: Accelerating Learning for All breaks the shackles of the industrial education system, revolutionizing the school experience through peer learning and hands-on activities. The ALfA program enables children and adults alike to learn foundational literacy and numeracy in months rather than years. Beyond reading, writing and arithmetic, ALfA builds the crucial life skills of collaboration, creativity, citizenship and character.

Both a classroom guide for teachers and an implementation roadmap for policymakers, this book explores a transformative approach to teaching and learning. Sprinkled with anecdotes from teachers, principals & students, Accelerating Learning for All inspires and equips us to build a better education system.”

DISRUPTIVE LITERACY

A Roadmap for Urgent Global Action
“A much-needed manifesto for achieving large scale transformation of the global literacy crisis.”

“Foundational literacy and numeracy are crucial to every person’s wellbeing. A nation’s literacy rate is a key predictor of its social and economic progress. Yet the world still has hundreds of millions of children in school but nor learning the basics, and adults deprived of these foundational skills. Why?

Drawing from remarkable examples of movements around the world, and sprinkled with stories from the authors’ grassroots educational work in the Global Dream Accelerating Learning for All (ALfA) program, Disruptive Literacy is an easy-to-read but hardto-ignore manifesto that will touch your heart and inspire you to action.”

INTERNATIONAL JURY

Fellowships and Awards are our way of saying: “Thank you for being a part of the change process so urgently needed in education.” Fellowships are for promising ideas for research and innovations-in-action. Awards are for programs which are already able to demonstrate their impact with evidence.

INTERNATIONAL JURY

An international jury will make the selections based on transparent criteria to award between 100 to 200 Fellowships this year depending upon the quality of the submissions.

FELLOWSHIPS

All GETI Fellows are expected to carry out their ideas for change post selection, and to gather evidence of their implementation on a regular basis in an e-portfolio. Evidence includes photos, videos, slide presentations, surveys, preand post-intervention data (quantitative, anecdotal and qualitative).

All GETI Fellows with evidence will automatically qualify for the Global Ed Innovator Awards.
